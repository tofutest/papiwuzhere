Artist Statement

I work primarily in a single medium — warm, nitrogen-rich, and produced entirely on-site. I do not sketch. I do not revise. Each piece emerges fully formed from a place deep within me, typically within the first thirty seconds of arriving at a location. My process is instinctual, immediate, and irreversible.

Critics have called my work "territorial." I prefer the term site-responsive. I am not marking objects. I am in dialogue with them. The rosemary bush speaks first. I answer. That is art.

The world is my studio, but here I bring you selected works from Papi's Atelier.

— Papi. Ballard.

🖼️ Masters Series

La Gioconda (After da Vinci)

Mixed media on canvas · Ballard · Spring 2026
Buy Print

She smiled at me. I am certain of it. There is a quality to that expression — knowing, patient, slightly smug — that I recognized immediately as a fellow creature who has gotten away with something. We understood each other at once. I was fully aware that this was not an original composition. Leonardo had arrived first by several centuries, and I made no attempt to conceal the influence. Great artists borrow. Great dogs copy directly. I approached the canvas with the gravity this subject deserved. My contribution is subtle but transformative warmth. I do not claim authorship. I claim improvement. You're welcome, Leonardo.

"She still smiles. If anything, more so." — Papi
Medium: Liquid on canvas  ·  Duration: Approximately 4 seconds  ·  Status: Signed and sealed

🖼️ Masters Series

American Gothic (After Grant Wood)

Mixed media on hardboard · Ballard · Spring 2026
Buy Print

I studied this painting for some time before I understood what was wrong with it. The composition is fine. The house is adequate. The pitchfork communicates something, though I am not sure what. These are not the problem.

The problem is the faces. Both of them — closed, flat, withheld. You look at this painting and you feel nothing has been offered. Nothing has been shared. The farmer and his daughter have mouths and are simply not using them correctly.

I corrected this. One small adjustment, freely given, from a place of genuine generosity. The tongue changes everything. It says: I am here. I am present. I have something to say and I am saying it with my whole face. Grant Wood painted duty. I painted joy.

"He forgot the tongue. I remembered it." — Papi
Medium: Tongue, sincerely deployed  ·  Duration: As long as it took  ·  Status: Complete
